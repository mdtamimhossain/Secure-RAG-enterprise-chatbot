# Codemars Corporation HR Leave Policy

Department: Human Resources
Audience: HR, managers, executives
Confidentiality: Restricted

This document is maintained by People Operations and is restricted to HR, managers, executives, and administrators.

## Medical Leave Review

HR reviews medical leave cases when an absence exceeds five consecutive business days. HR may request medical documentation through a secure HR case in PeopleHub.

## Sick Leave Entitlement

Full-time employees receive 10 paid sick days per calendar year. Part-time employees receive prorated sick leave based on scheduled weekly hours. Sick leave may be used for personal illness, injury, medical appointments, recovery after outpatient treatment, or preventive care.

Employees should notify their manager before the start of the workday when they need sick leave. If the employee is unable to contact the manager directly, they may notify HR through PeopleHub or ask a trusted colleague to notify the manager.

Sick leave is not paid out during offboarding and does not carry over into the next calendar year unless local law requires carry-over. HR must verify local statutory requirements before finalizing a country-specific sick leave case.

## Medical Certificate Rules

A medical certificate is required when sick leave exceeds 3 consecutive workdays. HR may request documentation earlier when there is a clear pattern of repeated short absences, but HR must handle the request privately and consistently.

Managers must not ask employees to reveal diagnosis details. Managers may only ask for expected return date, work restrictions, and whether urgent coverage is needed. Medical documents must be uploaded only to PeopleHub and must not be stored in team folders, email threads, Slack, Teams, or OrbitDocs.

## Extended Sick Leave

When an employee uses all 10 paid sick days, HR reviews whether extended medical leave, unpaid leave, disability coverage, local statutory leave, or workplace accommodation applies. Extended sick leave is reviewed case by case and requires HR approval.

Extended sick leave beyond 20 consecutive business days requires an HR case note, manager coverage plan, expected return review date, and confirmation that sensitive health information is stored separately from general personnel files.

## Return To Work

For absences longer than 10 consecutive business days, HR schedules a return-to-work check-in before the employee resumes regular duties. The check-in confirms any temporary work restrictions, phased return schedule, equipment needs, and manager coverage changes.

## Parental Leave

Eligible employees may receive up to 12 weeks of paid parental leave. HR confirms eligibility, records the leave schedule, and coordinates manager coverage planning.

## Annual Vacation Leave

Full-time employees receive 20 paid vacation days per calendar year. Vacation should be requested in PeopleHub at least 10 business days before the planned start date. Managers approve vacation based on team coverage, delivery commitments, and fairness across the team.

Employees may carry over up to 5 unused vacation days into the next calendar year. Carry-over vacation must be used by March 31 unless HR approves an exception for business-critical coverage or protected leave.

Vacation requests longer than 10 consecutive business days require manager approval and HR visibility. Vacation longer than 20 consecutive business days requires HR approval and a documented coverage plan.

## Emergency Leave

Employees may request up to 3 paid emergency leave days per calendar year for urgent family matters, household emergencies, sudden caregiving needs, or serious personal disruption. Emergency leave should be reported as soon as practical and recorded in PeopleHub after the employee returns.

Emergency leave is not intended for planned appointments, vacation, or recurring schedule conflicts. HR may approve additional unpaid emergency leave when circumstances are serious and documented.

## Bereavement Leave

Employees receive up to 5 paid bereavement leave days for the death of an immediate family member. Immediate family includes spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, sibling, grandparent, grandchild, or equivalent close family relationship.

Employees may request up to 2 paid bereavement leave days for extended family or close personal relationships. HR may approve additional unpaid time when travel, cultural observance, or estate responsibilities require more time.

## Public Holiday And Floating Day Rules

Employees observe public holidays based on their assigned work country. Employees who work in cross-border teams follow the holiday calendar in their employment contract unless HR approves a different calendar.

Employees receive 2 floating holidays per year. Floating holidays may be used for cultural, religious, civic, or personal observances. Floating holidays do not carry over and are not paid out at termination.

## Extended Leave Approval

Leave longer than 20 consecutive business days requires HR approval and manager acknowledgement. HR must document the business coverage plan before approval.

## Leave Misuse Review

HR may review leave misuse only when there is documented evidence of policy abuse, inconsistent records, falsified documentation, or a repeated pattern that materially affects work coverage. Managers must not investigate medical details directly.

Any corrective action related to leave misuse requires HR review, written documentation, and consistency with local employment law.

## Sensitive Records

Medical certificates, accommodation records, and protected leave documents must be stored only in PeopleHub. They must not be uploaded to OrbitDocs or shared in general chat channels.
